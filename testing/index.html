How to Prepare for Testing

Follow these steps to ensure a smooth testing experience

1

Gather Family History

Collect detailed medical history from both sides of your family, including information about genetic conditions, carrier status, and any previous testing results.

2

Schedule Genetic Counseling

Meet with a genetic counselor to discuss your family history, understand testing options, and learn about potential results and their implications.

3

Verify Insurance Coverage

Contact your insurance provider to understand coverage for genetic testing and counseling. Get pre-authorization if required.

4

Choose Testing Provider

Select a reputable testing center or laboratory that specializes in Jewish genetic conditions and offers comprehensive panels.

5

Complete the Test

Provide a blood or saliva sample as directed. The actual testing process is quick and minimally invasive.

6

Review Results

Meet with your genetic counselor to review results, understand their implications, and discuss next steps for you and your family.
